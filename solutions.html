Solar Panels

Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic (PV) cells. They're popular for residential and commercial energy production due to their sustainability and the abundant availability of sunlight.

Solar panels capture sunlight, and PV cells convert this into electricity through the photovoltaic effect. This electricity can either be used immediately, stored in batteries, or fed back into the grid.

Installation Cost: The cost varies significantly based on location, size of the system, and other factors, but a typical residential solar panel system can range from $15,000 to $25,000 before incentives and rebates.

Maintenance: Solar panels require minimal maintenance, mostly cleaning a few times a year and periodic checks by a professional to ensure everything is functioning correctly.

Lifespan: The average lifespan of solar panels is about 25 to 30 years, after which their efficiency may decrease but they can still produce electricity.

Wind Turbines

Wind turbines harness wind energy to generate electricity, offering a renewable and clean power source.

Wind turns the turbine blades, which spin a generator to produce electricity.

Installation Cost: Small wind turbines for residential use can cost between $10,000 to $70,000, depending on size and installation requirements.

Maintenance: Regular maintenance includes lubrication, inspection of blades and electrical components, and monitoring for optimal performance.

Lifespan: Well-maintained wind turbines can last 20 years or more, with some components like the tower lasting longer.

Geothermal Heat Pumps

Geothermal heat pumps use the stable temperature of the earth below the surface to heat and cool homes efficiently.

Heat pumps transfer heat between the ground and the home, providing heating in winter and cooling in summer.

Installation Cost: Installation costs range from $10,000 to $25,000, depending on system size and complexity.

Maintenance: Geothermal heat pumps have fewer moving parts compared to traditional HVAC systems, resulting in lower maintenance needs. Regular inspections and filter changes are recommended.

Lifespan: Geothermal heat pumps can last 20 to 25 years for the internal components, while the ground loop can last 50 years or more.

Hydroelectric Power

Hydroelectric power systems generate electricity by harnessing the energy of flowing water, such as rivers or streams.

Water flowing through turbines spins them to generate electricity, which is then transmitted to homes.

Installation Cost: Installation costs for small-scale hydroelectric systems can range from $10,000 to $50,000 or more, depending on the site's characteristics.

Maintenance: Hydroelectric systems require regular maintenance of turbines, electrical components, and water intake structures.

Lifespan: Well-maintained hydroelectric systems can last 50 years or more, with some components needing replacement or refurbishment over time.
